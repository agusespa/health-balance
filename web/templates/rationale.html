How it works

Biological Reserve Score (BRS)

A quantitative model measuring the balance between physiological entropy and lifestyle inputs.

The Master Algorithm

The system is built on the interaction between two opposing forces: Exponential Decay and Performance.

The Aging Tax (Entropy)

$$\text{Tax} = \text{Score} \times \frac{(\text{Age}^2 / 8000)}{52}$$

This formula reflects the accelerating nature of biological decay. Unlike linear models, a quadratic approach ensures that the "cost" of maintaining your health reserve starts low in youth but compounds significantly as you age.

The 8000 constant is calibrated to apply a ~20% annual decay rate at age 40, requiring higher consistency to stay level as you move into mid-life.

The Stability Buffer

Structural metrics like VO2 Max and WHtR act as a mathematical "floor." Because these biomarkers are slow to change, they provide a weighted anchor that protects your score from wild fluctuations based on a single poor week.

This ensures your score remains grounded in actual physical capacity, even as it tracks daily habits. For a 40-year-old, the persistent ~3.8 point weekly tax (per 1,000) creates a headwind that requires consistent performance to overcome.

Basic Health Pillar

Measuring the efficiency of internal repair and metabolic state.

  • Sleep Quality: Uses a baseline of 75. Deltas represent a "repair surplus" (positive) or "repair deficit" (negative). Consistent high-quality sleep is the primary driver of systemic recovery.
  • Structural Ratio (WHtR): A high-weight anchor. Maintaining a Waist-to-Height ratio below 0.48 is critical for preventing metabolic inflammation and visceral fat accumulation.
  • Resting Heart Rate (RHR): A relative comparison against your 3-month rolling average. Sustained increases often signal overtraining, illness, or chronic stress.
  • Nutrition Quality: A self-reported 1-10 index. Scores above 7 represent an anti-inflammatory diet, fueling the Biological Reserve rather than depleting it.

Fitness Pillar

Measuring cardiovascular ceiling and structural resilience.

  • VO2 Max: The most heavily weighted function. As the strongest statistical predictor of lifespan, it represents your body's ability to deliver oxygen to tissues under stress.
  • Cardio Recovery: Measures the 60s heart rate drop after vigorous effort (70%+ max HR). A drop of 25+ BPM indicates a robust "parasympathetic brake," a key marker of a youthful autonomic nervous system.
  • Frequency Metrics: Steps (8k baseline), Workouts (HIIT/Strength), and Mobility sessions act as "operational inputs"—the daily effort required to overcome the Aging Tax.

Cognition Pillar

Measuring neural efficiency and mental capacity.

  • Mindfulness: Active cortisol management. Regular practice protects the brain from the neurotoxic effects of chronic stress and preserves hippocampal volume.
  • Cognitive Novelty: Measures time spent in "Deep Learning" states. Learning complex new symbolic systems (music notation/foreign syntax) builds Cognitive Reserve, creating a buffer against age-related neurodegeneration.
  • Working Memory (Dual N-Back): Calculated as a deviation from Level 2. Training working memory improves "fluid intelligence"—the ability to solve new problems, which typically declines with age.
  • Reaction Speed: Identifies acute neurological fatigue. Slowing reaction times are often the first sign of age-related neuromuscular decline.

Disclaimer

The Biological Reserve Score (BRS) is a motivational tool designed for general health tracking and lifestyle optimization. It is a mathematical model, not a medical diagnostic. These metrics do not replace professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always consult with a healthcare provider before making significant changes to your exercise or nutrition routine.
