How it works

Biological Reserve Score (BRS)

A quantitative model measuring the balance between physiological entropy and lifestyle inputs.

The Master Algorithm: Accelerating Entropy

The system is built on the interaction between two opposing forces: Exponential Decay and Performance.

The Aging Tax (Entropy)

$$\text{Tax} = \text{Current Score} \times \frac{(\text{Age}^2 / 8000)}{52}$$

Unlike linear models, this formula reflects the accelerating nature of biological entropy. As your age increases, the cost of maintenance grows quadratically, requiring higher consistency to preserve your reserve.

The Stability Buffer

High-value metrics like VO2 Max and WHtR act as filters. Being slow to change, they provide a mathematical "floor" that protects your score from temporary lifestyle fluctuations.

Basic Health Pillar

Measuring the efficiency of internal repair and metabolic state.

  • Sleep Variance: Uses a baseline of 75. Deltas represent a "repair surplus" (positive) or "repair deficit" (negative).
  • Structural Ratio (WHtR): A high-weight static variable. Small improvements in body composition relative to height result in significant long-term score gains.
  • Resting Heart Rate (RHR): A relative comparison against your 3-month rolling average to detect systemic strain.

Fitness Pillar

Measuring cardiovascular ceiling and structural resilience.

  • VO2 Max (x20): Our most heavily weighted function. Uses Age-Adjusted Baselines to keep the score relevant to your current physiological potential.
  • Cardio Recovery: Measures heart rate drop in 60s against a 20 BPM baseline, capturing the nervous system's transition to a restful state.

Cognition Pillar

Measuring neural efficiency and mental capacity.

  • Working Memory (Dual N-Back): Calculated as a deviation from Level 2. Cognitive contribution grows linearly as your "N-Level" increases.
  • Reaction Speed: Measures the difference between a 3-month baseline and current performance to identify acute neurological fatigue.
  • Mindfulness Frequency: A frequency-based function that rewards consistency in active stress regulation.
